Cameron Liddell

> Your friendly neighbourhood developer.

I am a dedicated and passionate programmer with experience in a multitude of languages, and technologies. I first started being interested in programming somewhere around the ages of thirteen where I was first introduced to HTML and CSS by a friend of mine. He explained that I could let my creative juices flow with making websites, and create anything I wanted. I quickly picked it up, only to find I preferred to work on software. I finished school at 16, and then followed that with a couple of years of working. I didn't want to do anything else, just work and make money. However, at the age of 21, I decided that instead of wasting my skills with computers I would do something about it. After spending 4 to 5 years working, I needed a change. I signed up to my local college and started the following September, doing Level 3 computing. I got quickly invested into the course and thoroughly enjoyed it, spending countless hours working on assignments to make them the best I could. The act of going to college also re-ignited my love for programming. I picked C# up and starting developing lots of random things. After a couple of months of figuring out the ropes, I decided to set myself a proper project. So, I decided to do a Discord Bot as this implemented some key features such as MySQL, API usage and implementing OOP. These bots are just for responding to certain messages when a user uses a specific prefix at the start of the message. After many hours of thinking, I decided to create a bot that did lots of random things. Random space facts, cat images and much more. CamBot was born, and I immediately started working on him. The only thing with Discord bots that is frustrating sometimes is the need to host the bot somewhere. I decided that I would dive into the deep end and purchase a Raspberry Pi 4 and host it from in my bedroom. I received the package, and started to test and experiment with ways in which to host the bot on there. After many hours, he was finally working. Since then, I have started enjoying writing Java for Minecraft plugins, and therefore, sadly CamBot has not been touched much, only receiving minor updates at any given time.

Working with Java is fun, but working on a game you love with that language is even more fun. I thoroughly enjoy learning the mechanics of the game and fleshing them out, while also bending the rules to make some really interesting designs! I've learnt a lot through Java such as using MySQL databases with JDBC, SQLite for local storage, YAML configuration files, Apache Maven, and of course, Java. I have since joined a company called Songoda of whom are a premium Minecraft plugin marketplace and I am on the Customer relations team. However, their CEO, Brianna, has taken me under her wing and has been mentoring me weekly, giving me ideas, tips and tricks and teaching me the fundamentals of working with Java in this environment. My goal for working with Java is to end up on the Songoda Marketplace's development team, working alongside Brianna and the rest of the team as well as creating my own independant plugins for other marketplaces such as Spigot and McMarket.

As mentioned before, I am currently at college studying a level 3 computing course, with hopes to go to University. I am an aspiring Computer Game programmer, with dreams of working on triple-A games after my university course of which is a 4 year sandwich course. I chose a sandwich year in the hopes of getting good experience for after University when I eventually end up in the job market.

CamBot is the em-bot-diment (get it?) of random! He takes APIs from across the globe with the main function of randomising everything inside. He contains APIs from NASA, random dog pictures, random meals and cocktails and even a couple of dad-jokes sprinkled in! Basically, if it's random, CamBot has it! Alongside lots of random things, he also has some helpful features like the weather, cryptocurrency prices and a scoreboard system so you can compete to be the top user! Written in C#, the bot is built for Discord. With this language comes efficiency and diversity that are a must for projects like these! He currently runs off of a Raspberry Pi 4 in my bedroom!

MetroCore is a Minecraft plugin with one aspect in mind: Usability. With the need for Minecraft moderation systems to be more efficient at the times go on. With features such as bug reports, player reports, and suggestions, both regular players and admins are only a single click away from reforming the community we love! Written in Java with a hint of SQLite, the plugin is fully compatible with the latest versions of Minecraft, allowing for maximum access.

Metrocity is a Minecraft server aimed at creating a beautiful, loving community within the wonderful world of blocks! But what is my specific job? I am the lead developer in this community, ensuring that everything is running smoothly. I create custom plugins, work with SQLite, MySQL and I configurate plugins to create a bug free, enjoyable environment for everyone to play! An example of something I have created for Metrocity is "MetroCore", as seen above. This is the main plugin that runs the administration side of things, with many more to come!

MobContracts is a Minecraft plugin built to create epic boss battles, and amazing fight scenes! With the combination of Java, MySQL and SQLite, the code behind this plugin is efficient and executes flawlessly. Firstly, the player starts a contract, with it's boosted stats, and special area-of-effect effects, the player then fights the contract to the death! With a fully functioning reward system, as well as a leaderboard the players have everything they need to be fully competitive! Using Lambda expressions and ternary to create well executed, legible code and the use of a MySQL connection pool called HikariCP allows for fast database connections to ensure maximum efficiency.
